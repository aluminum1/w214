Example 2.5.5

In our running example, we see from (2.5.3) and (2.5.4) that

\begin{equation*} [\ve{b}_1]_\basis{C} = \left[ \begin{array}{c} 1 \\ 3 \end{array} \right], \quad [\ve{b}_2]_\basis{C} = \left[ \begin{array}{c} 2 \\ 3 \end{array} \right] \, \text{.} \end{equation*}

Hence the change-of-basis matrix from \(\basis{B}\) to \(\basis{C}\) is

\begin{equation*} \mat{P}_{\basis{C} \leftarrow \basis{B}} = \begin{bmatrix} 1 & 2 \\ 3 & 3 \end{bmatrix}\text{.} \end{equation*}
