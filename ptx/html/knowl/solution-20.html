Solution 2.3.16.1

  1. Suppose

    \begin{equation*} (a,2a,-a) = (1,0,5) \text{.} \end{equation*}

    Then the first entry requires that \(a =1 \) but the second entry requires that \(a = 0 \text{.}\) Hence there can be no \(a \) satisfying the equation.

  2. \begin{equation*} 2 \ve v_1 + 3 \ve v_2 = 2(1,2,-1) + 3(1,0,5) \\ = (2 + 3, 4 + 0, -2 + 15) = (5,4,13) = \ve v_4 \end{equation*}

  3. Suppose

    \begin{equation*} a \ve v_1 + b \ve v_4 = \ve v_6 \end{equation*}

    i.e.

    \begin{equation*} (a,2a,-a) + (b,0,5b)= (1,1,0). \end{equation*}

    Consideration of the second entry gives \(a = \frac{1}{2} \) which, by looking at the first entry, forces \(b = \frac{1}{2}\) but then, if we look at the third entry, \(-\frac{1}{2} + \frac{5}{2} \neq 0 \text{.}\) Hence there are no solutions to the equation.
