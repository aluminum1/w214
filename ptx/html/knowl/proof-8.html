Proof

Start with the original spanning list of vectors

\begin{equation} \basis{S} = \left\{ \,\, \ve{s}_1, \ve{s}_2, \ldots, \ve{s}_n \right\}\tag{2.2.10} \end{equation}

and consider the ‘bloated’ list

\begin{equation} \basis{S}' = \left\{\ve{l}_1, \ve{s}_1, \ve{s}_2, \cdots, \ve{s}_n \right\}\tag{2.2.11} \end{equation}

Now, since \(\basis{S}\) spans \(V\text{,}\) we know that \(\ve{l}_1\) can be written as a linear combination of the vectors \(\ve{s}_1, \ldots, \ve{s}_n\text{.}\) Therefore, by Item 2 of Proposition 2.2.8, we know that \(\basis{S}'\) is linearly dependent. Thus, by Item 3 of Proposition 2.2.8, either:

  • \(\ve{l}_1 = \ve{0}\text{.}\) This cannot be true, since then \(\basis{L}\) would be linearly dependent by Remark 2.2.2, contradicting our initial assumption.
  • or one of the \(\ve{s}\)-vectors, say \(\ve{s}_r\text{,}\) can be expressed as a linear combination of the preceding vectors. We can then remove \(\ve{s}_r\) from the list \(\basis{S}'\) (‘bump it off’), and the resulting list
    \begin{equation} \basis{S}_1 := \left\{\ve{l}_1, \ve{s}_1, \ve{s}_2, \cdots, \hat{\ve{s}_r}, \cdots, \ve{s}_n \right\} \qquad \mbox{(\(\ve{s}_r\) omitted)}\tag{2.2.12} \end{equation}
    will still span \(V\text{,}\) by Exercise 2.1.7.

We can go on in this way, each time transferring another one of the \(\ve{l}\)-vectors into the list, and removing another one of the \(\ve{s}\)-vectors, and still have a list which spans \(V\text{:}\)

\begin{align*} \basis{L} \amp = \bopen \ve{l}_1, \ldots, \ve{l}_m \bclose \amp \basis{S} \amp = \bopen \ve{s}_1, \ldots, \ve{s}_n \bclose \\ \basis{L}_1 \amp = \bopen \ve{l}_2, \ldots, \ve{l}_m \bclose \amp \basis{S}_1 \amp = \bopen \ve{l}_1, \underbrace{\ve{s}_1, \ldots, \ve{s}_n}_{n-1} \bclose \\ \basis{L}_2 \amp = \bopen \ve{l}_3, \ldots, \ve{l}_m \bclose \amp \basis{S}_2 \amp = \bopen \ve{l}_2, \ve{l}_1, \underbrace{\ve{s}_1, \ldots, \ve{s}_n}_{n-2} \bclose \\ \amp \vdots \amp \amp \vdots \end{align*}

Now, suppose that \(m > n\text{.}\) When we reach the \(n\)th stage of this process, we will have \(\basis{S}_n = \bopen \ve{l}_n, \ldots, \ve{l}_1 \bclose\text{,}\) and it will span \(V\text{.}\) Therefore, in particular, \(\ve{l}_{n+1}\) (which we know exists, since \(m > n \)) will be a linear combination of \(\ve{l}_1, \ldots, \ve{l}_n\text{.}\) But then, by Item 2 of Proposition 2.2.8, we conclude that \(\basis{L}\) is linearly dependent. But we were told in the beginning that \(\basis{L}\) is linearly independent, so we have a contradiction. Hence, our assumption that \(m > n\) must be false. Therefore, we must have \(m \leq n\text{.}\)
