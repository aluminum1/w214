We check Item 3 of Proposition 2.2.8. That is, we check:

  • Is \(\ve{f}_1 = \ve{0}\text{?}\) No.

  • Is \(\ve{f}_2\) is a scalar multiple of \(\ve{f}_1\text{?}\) No.

  • Is \(\ve{f}_3\) is a linear combination of \(\ve{f}_1\) and \(\ve{f}_2\text{?}\) Yes, since

    \begin{equation*} \ve{f}_3 = - \ve{f}_1 + \ve{f}_2. \end{equation*}

    Hence, \(\basis{B}\) is linearly dependent.
