Example 1.6.21 Trigonometric polynomials

A trigonometric polynomial is a function \(\ve{T} : \mathbb{R} \rightarrow \mathbb{R}\) of the form

\begin{equation} \ve{T} (x) = a_0 + \sum_{k=1}^n a_k \cos(kx) + \sum_{k=1}^n b_k \sin(kx).\tag{1.6.7} \end{equation}

The degree of a trigonometric polynomial is the highest multiple of \(x\) which occurs inside one of the sines or cosines in its formula. For instance,

\begin{equation*} 3 - \cos(x) + 6 \sin(3x) \end{equation*}

is a trigonometric polynomial of degree 3. We write the set of all trigonometric polynomials as \(\Trig\) and the set of all trigonometric polynomials having degree less than or equal to \(n\) as \(\Trig_n\text{.}\)
