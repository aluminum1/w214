We saw in Example 2.2.7 that the list of vectors \(\ve{f}_1 = (-1, \, 2)\text{,}\) \(\ve{f}_2 = (1, \,1)\text{,}\) \(\ve{f}_3 = (2, \, -1)\) in \(\mathbb{R}^3\) is linearly dependent. Therefore, by Proposition 2.2.10, we must have either:

  • \(\ve{f}_1 = \ve{0}\) (not the case), or

  • \(\ve{f}_2\) is a scalar multiple of \(\ve{f}_1\) (not the case), or

  • \(\ve{f}_3\) is a linear combination of \(\ve{f}_1\) and \(\ve{f}_2\text{,}\) which is true:

    \begin{equation*} \ve{f}_3 = - \ve{f}_1 + \ve{f}_2 \end{equation*}
