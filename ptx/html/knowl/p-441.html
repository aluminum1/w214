If \(\basis{B}_2\) does not span \(W\text{,}\) then there exists a vector \(\ve{w}_3 \in W\) which is not a linear combination of \(\ve{w}_1\) and \(\ve{w}_2\text{.}\) Now consider the list \(\basis{B}_3 = \left\{ \ve{w}_1, \ve{w}_2, \ve{w}_3 \right\}\text{.}\) Again, \(\basis{B}_3\) is linearly independent, by Item 3 of Proposition 2.2.8.
