In the same way, we can talk about 3-variable polynomials, and so on, eg.

\begin{equation*} r = 5x^3y^2z + 3xy - 4xz^3 \end{equation*}

is a polynomial of total degree \(6\) in the variables \(x,y,z\text{.}\) We can regard a polynomial in \(k\) variables as a special kind of function

\begin{equation*} r : \mathbb{R}^k \rightarrow \mathbb{R}. \end{equation*}

We write \(\Poly^k\) for the vector space of all \(k\)-variable polynomials. We can regard \(\Poly^k\) as a subspace of the vector space \(\Fun(\mathbb{R}^k)\) of all functions on \(\mathbb{R}^k\text{.}\) Similary, we write \(\Poly^k_n\) for the vector space of all \(k\)-variable polynomials of total degree less than or equal to \(n\text{,}\) and we regard it too as a subspace of \(\Fun(\mathbb{R}^n)\text{.}\)
