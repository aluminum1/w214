Example 1.6.20 Polynomials in many variables

A monomial in two variables \(x\) and \(y\) is an expression of the form \(x^m y^n\) for some nonnegative integers \(m,n\text{.}\) The total degree of the monomial is \(m+n\text{.}\)For example,

\begin{equation*} x^3 y^2, xy^7, \quad \mbox{and } x^3 \end{equation*}

are monomials in the variables \(x\) and \(y\) of total degree

\begin{equation*} 5, 8, \quad \mbox{and } 3 \end{equation*}

respectively. A polynomial in two variables is a linear combination of monomials. The degree of the polynomial is the highest total degree of the monomials occuring in the linear combination. For instance,

\begin{equation} p = 5x^3 y^2 - 3xy^7, \quad q = xy^2 -x^3 + 3y^2\tag{1.6.6} \end{equation}

are polynomials in two variables with total degree \(8\) and \(3\) respectively.

We write \(\Poly^2\) for the set of all polynomials in two variables, and \(\Poly^2_n\) for the set of all two-variable polynomials of total degree less than or equal to \(n\text{.}\) For instance, consier the polynomial

\begin{equation*} p = 5x^3 y^2 - 3xy^7 \end{equation*}

The total degree of \(p\) is 8. So:

  • \(p \in \Poly^2\text{,}\)
  • \(p \in \Poly^2_8\)
  • \(p \in \Poly^2_12\)
  • \(p \ni \Poly_2_7\)

We can regard a polynomial \(p\) in two variables as a special kind of function

\begin{equation*} p : \mathbb{R}^2 \rightarrow \mathbb{R}. \end{equation*}

In this way, we can regard \(\Poly_2\) (and similarly \(\Poly^2_n\)) as a subset of the vector space \(\Fun(\mathbb{R}^2)\) of all functions on \(\mathbb{R}^2\text{.}\) Indeed, they are subspaces, as the reader will check.

Two polynomials \(p\) and \(q\) in variables \(x\) and \(y\) are defined to be equal if and only if all their corresponding coefficients are equal. This is equivalent to the statement that \(p(x,y)=q(x,y)\) for all \((x,y) \in \mathbb{R}^2\text{.}\)

In the same way, we can talk about 3-variable polynomials, and so on, eg.

\begin{equation*} r = 5x^3y^2z + 3xy - 4xz^3 \end{equation*}

is a polynomial of total degree \(6\) in the variables \(x,y,z\text{.}\) We can regard a polynomial in \(k\) variables as a special kind of function

\begin{equation*} r : \mathbb{R}^k \rightarrow \mathbb{R}. \end{equation*}

We write \(\Poly^k\) for the vector space of all \(k\)-variable polynomials. We can regard \(\Poly^k\) as a subspace of the vector space \(\Fun(\mathbb{R}^k)\) of all functions on \(\mathbb{R}^k\text{.}\) Similary, we write \(\Poly^k_n\) for the vector space of all \(k\)-variable polynomials of total degree less than or equal to \(n\text{,}\) and we regard it too as a subspace of \(\Fun(\mathbb{R}^n)\text{.}\)
