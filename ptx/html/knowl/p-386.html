Now, suppose that \(m > n\text{.}\) When we reach the \(n\)th stage of this process, we will have \(\basis{S}_n = \bopen \ve{l}_n, \ldots, \ve{l}_1 \bclose\text{,}\) and it will span \(V\text{.}\) Therefore, in particular, \(\ve{l}_{n+1}\) (which we know exists, since \(m > n \)) will be a linear combination of \(\ve{l}_1, \ldots, \ve{l}_n\text{.}\) But then, by Item 2 of Proposition 2.2.8, we conclude that \(\basis{L}\) is linearly dependent. But we were told in the beginning that \(\basis{L}\) is linearly independent, so we have a contradiction. Hence, our assumption that \(m > n\) must be false. Therefore, we must have \(m \leq n\text{.}\)
