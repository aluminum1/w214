\(\Mat_{2,2}\) has dimension 4 since it is spanned by \(\ve e_{1,1}, \ve e_{1,2}, \ve e_{2,1}, \ve e_{2,2} \) where \(\ve e_{i,j}\) is the matrix with a \(1 \) in the \(ij^\text{th} \) entry and 0's everywhere else. By Exercise 2.3.2.2(a), any linearly indepedent list of vectors in \(\Mat_{2,2}\) has length at most 4. Since the list of matrices in \(\Mat_{2,2}\) in Exercise 2.2.2 has length 5, it cannot be linearly independent.
