The list of vectors in \(\Mat_{2,2}\) given by

\begin{equation*} \ve{v}_1 = \begin{bmatrix} 1 & 2 \\ 1 & 1 \end{bmatrix},\, \ve{v}_2 = \begin{bmatrix} 1 & 0 \\ -2 & 1 \end{bmatrix},\, \ve{v}_3 = \begin{bmatrix} 1 & 0 \\ 2 & 3 \end{bmatrix},\, \ve{v}_4 = \begin{bmatrix} 0 & 3 \\ 1 & -1 \end{bmatrix},\, \ve{v}_5 = \begin{bmatrix} 1 & 0 \\ 0 & 1 \end{bmatrix} \end{equation*}

is linearly dependent (you will prove this in Exercise 2.3.5.4, but for the sake of this question you may assume it to be true). Go through the same steps as in Example 2.2.9 to find the first vector in the list which is either the zero vector or a linear combination of the preceding vectors.
