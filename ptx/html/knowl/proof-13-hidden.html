Since \(V\) is finite-dimensional, it has a basis \(\ve{e}_1, \ldots, \ve{e}_n\text{.}\) Now consider the list

\begin{equation*} L : \ve{v}_1, \ve{v}_2, \ldots, \ve{v}_k, \ve{e}_1, \ve{e}_2, \ldots, \ve{e}_n \end{equation*}

which clearly spans \(V\text{.}\) By sifting this list, we will arrive at a basis for \(V\text{,}\) by LemmaÂ 2.3.21. Some of the \(\ve{e}\)-vectors may have been removed. But none of the \(\ve{v}\)-vectors will have been removed, since that would mean some \(\ve{v}_i\) is a linear combination of the preceding vectors \(\ve{v}_1, \ldots, \ve{v}_{i-1}\text{,}\) which is impossible, as \(\ve{v}_1, \ldots, \ve{v}_k\) is linearly independent list. Hence after sifting the list \(L\) we indeed extend our original list \(\ve{v}_1, \ldots, \ve{v}_k\) to a basis of \(V\text{.}\)
