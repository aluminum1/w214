Item 2.3.2.8.a
We omit the check that \(V \) is a subspace of \(\Poly_2 \) since it is routine. Let us now construct a basis for \(V \text{.}\) Begin with a non-zero vector \(\ve v_1 \) in \(V \text{.}\) We make the most obvious choice, let \(\ve v_1 = x -2 \text{.}\) Next pick any vector \(\ve v_2 \) in \(V \) not in the span of \(\left \{ \ve v_1 \right\} \text{.}\) An obvious choice is \(\ve v_2 = x(x-2) \text{.}\) Since \(V \) is not all of \(\Poly_2 \text{,}\) we know that \(\dim V \lt 3 \text{.}\) Thus \(\basis B' = \bopen \ve v_1, \ve v_2 \bclose\) is a basis for \(V \) and so \(\dim V = 2 \text{.}\)
