Proof

(Proof of \(\Leftarrow\)). Suppose \(k=0\text{.}\) Then \(k \smul\ve{v} = 0 \smul\ve{v} = \ve{0}\) by R8 of a vector space. On the other hand, suppose \(\ve{v} = \ve{0}\text{.}\) Then \(k \smul\ve{v} = k \smul\ve{0} = \ve{0}\) by Exercise 1.5.2.

(Proof of \(\Rightarrow\)). Suppose \(k \smul\ve{v} = \ve{0}\text{.}\) There are two possibilities: either \(k = 0\text{,}\) or \(k \neq 0\text{.}\) If \(k=0\text{,}\) then we are done. If \(k \neq 0\text{,}\) then \(\frac{1}{k}\) exists and we can multiply both sides by it:

\begin{align*} k \smul\ve{v} \amp = \ve{0}\\ \therefore \frac{1}{k} \smul(k \smul\ve{v}) \amp = \frac{1}{k} \smul\ve{0} \amp \amp \mbox{(Multiplied both sides by \(\frac{1}{k}\))}\\ \therefore \left(\frac{1}{k} k\right) \smul\ve{v} \amp = \ve{0} \amp \amp \mbox{(On the LHS, we used R6. On the RHS, we used } \knowl{./knowl/mult_by_zero_ex.html}{\text{Exercise 1.5.2}})\\ \therefore 1 \smul\ve{v} \amp = \ve{0} \amp \amp \mbox{(using \(\frac{1}{k} k = 1\))}\\ \therefore \ve{v} \amp = \ve{0} \amp \amp \mbox{(R7)} \end{align*}

Hence in the case \(k \neq 0\) we must have \(\ve{v} = \ve{0}\text{,}\) which is what we wanted to show.
