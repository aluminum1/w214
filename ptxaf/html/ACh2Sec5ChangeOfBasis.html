Afdeling 2.5 Basisverandering

¶

Onderafdeling 2.5.1 Koördinaatvektore verskil in verskillende basisse

Veronderstel dat \(\basis{B} = \left\{ \ve{b}_1, \ve{b}_2 \right\}\) en \(\basis{C} = \left\{ \ve{c}_1, \ve{c}_2 \right\}\) twee verskillende basisse vir \(\mathbb{R}^2\) is, geïllustreer soos volg:

Veronderstel ons word 'n vektor \(\ve{w} \in \mathbb{R}^2\) gegee:

Ons wil die koördinaatvektor van dieselfde vektor \(\ve{w}\) relatief tot twee verskillende basisse \(\basis{B}\) en \(\basis{C}\) bereken.

Met hierdie spesifieke vektor \(\ve{w}\text{,}\) uit Figuur 2.5.1(a) sien ons dat in die basis \(\basis{B}\) het ons

\begin{equation} \ve{w} = -3 \ve{b}_1 + 2 \ve{b}_2 \qquad \therefore \, [\ve{w}]_\basis{B} = \left[\begin{array}{c} -3 \\ 2 \end{array}\right] \,\text{.}\tag{2.5.1} \end{equation}

Aan die anderkant, in basis \(\basis{C}\) het ons

\begin{equation} \ve{w} = \ve{c}_1 - 3\ve{c}_2 \qquad \therefore \, [\ve{w}]_\basis{C} = \left[\begin{array}{c} 1 \\ -3 \end{array}\right]\text{.}\label{correct_exp_w}\tag{2.5.2} \end{equation}

So dieselfde vektor \(\ve{w}\) het verskillende koördinaatvektore \([\ve{w}]_\basis{B}\) en \([\ve{w}]_\basis{C}\) relatief tot die basisse \(\basis{B}\) en \(\basis{C}\text{!}\)

Onderafdeling 2.5.2 Omskakeling van een basis na 'n ander

Veronderstel nou ons het slegs \([\ve{w}]_\basis{B}\text{,}\) die koördinaatvektor van \(\ve{w}\) in basis \(\basis{B}\text{.}\) Met ander woorde, veronderstel ons weet net dat

\begin{equation*} [\ve{w}]_\basis{B} = \left[ \begin{array}{c} -3 \\ 2 \end{array}\right]\text{,} \end{equation*}

dit is, \(\ve{w} = -3 \ve{b}_1 + 2 \ve{b}_2\text{.}\) Hoe kan ons \([\ve{w}]_\basis{C}\text{,}\) die koördinaatvektor van \(\ve{w}\) in die basis \(\basis{C}\) bereken?

Die beste benadering is om elke vektor in \(\basis{B}\) as 'n lineêre kombinasie van die basisvektore in \(\basis{C}\) uit te druk. In die volgende figuur word die vektore \(\ve{b}_1\) en \(\ve{b}_2\) uitgebeeld teen die agtergrond van die basis \(\basis{C}\text{:}\)

Ons lees af dat:

\begin{align} \ve{b}_1 \amp = \ve{c}_1 + 3 \ve{c}_2\label{b_in_terms_of_c}\tag{2.5.3}\\ \ve{b}_2 \amp = 2 \ve{c}_1 + 3 \ve{c}_2\label{b_in_terms_of_c_2}\tag{2.5.4} \end{align}

Daarom bereken ons:

\begin{align*} \ve{w} \amp = -3 \ve{b}_1 + 2 \ve{b}_2\\ \amp = -3 (\ve{c}_1 + 3 \ve{c}_2) + 2(2 \ve{c}_1 + 3 \ve{c}_2)\\ \amp = \ve{c}_1 - 3 \ve{c}_2 \end{align*}

Hiervan lees ons af dat

\begin{equation} [\ve{w}]_\basis{C} = \left[\begin{array}{c} 1 \\ -3 \end{array}\right]\label{w_in_basis_C}\tag{2.5.5} \end{equation}

Wat die korrekte antwoord is, soos ons weet uit (2.5.2).

Hierdie berekening kan in terme van matrikse uitgedruk word.

Definisie 2.5.4

Laat \(\basis{B} = \left\{ \ve{b}_1, \ldots, \ve{b}_n \right\}\) en \(\basis{C} = \left\{ \ve{c}_1, \ldots, \ve{c}_n \right\}\) basisse vir 'n vektorruimte \(V\) wees. Die basisomskakelingsmatriks van \(\basis{B}\) na \(\basis{C}\) is die \(n \times n\)-matriks waarvan die kolomme die koördinaatvektore \([\ve{b}_1]_\basis{C}, \ldots, [\ve{b}_n]_\basis{C}\) is:

\begin{equation*} \mat{P}_{\basis{C} \leftarrow \basis{B}} := \begin{bmatrix} \left[\begin{array}{c} \, \\ \ve{b}_1 \\ \, \end{array}\right]_\basis{C} & \left[\begin{array}{c} \,\\ \ve{b}_2 \\ \, \end{array}\right]_\basis{C} & \ldots & \left[\begin{array}{c} \, \\ \ve{b}_n \\ \,\end{array}\right]_\basis{C} \end{bmatrix}\, \text{.} \end{equation*}
Voorbeeld 2.5.5

In die deurlopende voorbeeld, sien ons uit (2.5.3) en (2.5.4) dat

\begin{equation*} [\ve{b}_1]_\basis{C} = \left[ \begin{array}{c} 1 \\ 3 \end{array} \right], \quad [\ve{b}_2]_\basis{C} = \left[ \begin{array}{c} 2 \\ 3 \end{array} \right] \, \text{.} \end{equation*}

Daarom is die basisomskakelingsmatriks van \(\basis{B}\) na \(\basis{C}\)

\begin{equation*} \mat{P}_{\basis{C} \leftarrow \basis{B}} = \begin{bmatrix} 1 & 2 \\ 3 & 3 \end{bmatrix}\text{.} \end{equation*}

Voordat ons verder gaan, hersien ons 'n aspek van matriksvermenigvuldiging. Veronderstel ons groepeer \(m\) kolomvektore saam om 'n matriks te vorm:

\begin{equation*} \begin{bmatrix} \left[\begin{array}{c} \, \\ \text{C}_1 \\ \, \end{array}\right] & \left[\begin{array}{c} \,\\ \text{C}_2 \\ \, \end{array}\right] & \ldots & \left[\begin{array}{c} \, \\ \text{C}_m \\ \,\end{array}\right] \end{bmatrix} \end{equation*}

(Ons basisomskakelingsmatriks \(\mat{P}_{\basis{C} \leftarrow \basis{B}}\) is so gevorm.) Dan word die produk van hierdie matriks met 'n kolomvektor soos volg bereken:

\begin{equation*} \begin{bmatrix} \left[\begin{array}{c} \, \\ \text{C}_1 \\ \, \end{array}\right] & \left[\begin{array}{c} \,\\ \text{C}_2 \\ \, \end{array}\right] & \ldots & \left[\begin{array}{c} \, \\ \text{C}_m \\ \,\end{array}\right] \end{bmatrix} \end{equation*}

(For instance, our change-of-basis matrix \(\mat{P}_{\basis{C} \leftarrow \basis{B}}\) was formed in this way.) Then the product of this matrix with a column vector can be computed as follows:

\begin{equation} \begin{bmatrix} \left[\begin{array}{c} \, \\ \text{C}_1 \\ \, \end{array}\right] & \left[\begin{array}{c} \,\\ \text{C}_2 \\ \, \end{array}\right] & \ldots & \left[\begin{array}{c} \, \\ \text{C}_m \\ \,\end{array}\right] \end{bmatrix}\left[\begin{array}{c} a_1 \\ a_2 \\ \vdots \\ a_m \end{array}\right] = a_1 \left[\begin{array}{c} \, \\ \text{C}_1 \\ \, \end{array}\right] + a_2 \left[\begin{array}{c} \, \\ \text{C}_2 \\ \, \end{array}\right] + \cdots + a_m \left[\begin{array}{c} \, \\ \text{C}_m \\ \, \end{array}\right]\text{.}\label{column_vector_multiplication}\tag{2.5.6} \end{equation}
Verstaanpunt 2.5.6

Bewys die bostaande formule!

Ons kan nou die volgende stelling bewys.

Stelling 2.5.7 Basisomskakeling

Veronderstel dat \(\basis{B} = \left\{ \ve{b}_1, \ldots, \ve{b}_n\right\}\) en \(\basis{C} = \left\{ \ve{c}_1, \ldots, \ve{c}_n \right\}\) basisse vir 'n vektorruimte \(V\) is, en laat \(\mat{P}_{\basis{C} \leftarrow \basis{B}}\) die basiskomskakelingsmatriks van \(\basis{B}\) na \(\basis{C}\) wees. Dan, vir alle vektore \(\ve{v}\) in \(V\text{,}\)

\begin{equation} [\ve{v}]_\basis{C} = \mat{P}_{\basis{C} \leftarrow \basis{B}} [\ve{v}]_\basis{B} \text{.}\label{change-of-basis-in-action}\tag{2.5.7} \end{equation}
Bewys

Laat \(\ve{v} \in V\text{.}\) Brei \(\ve{v}\) uit in die basis \(\basis{B}\text{:}\)

\begin{equation*} \ve{v} = a_1 \ve{b}_1 + a_2 \ve{b}_2 + \cdots + a_n \ve{b}_n, \mbox{i.e. } [\ve{v}]_\basis{B} = \left[\begin{array}{c} a_1 \\ \vdots \\ a_n \end{array}\right]\text{.} \end{equation*}

Dan,

\begin{align*} _\basis{C} \amp = [a_1 \ve{b}_1 + \cdots + a_n \ve{b}_n]_\basis{C}\\ \amp = a_1 [\ve{b}_1]_\basis{C} + \cdots + a_n [\ve{b}_n]_\basis{C} \amp \amp (\knowl{./knowl/lin_of_coord_vectors.html}{\text{Lemma 2.4.8}})\\ \amp = \begin{bmatrix} \left[\begin{array}{c} \, \\ \ve{b}_1 \\ \, \end{array}\right] & \left[\begin{array}{c} \,\\ \ve{b}_2 \\ \, \end{array}\right] & \ldots & \left[\begin{array}{c} \, \\ \ve{b}_n \\ \,\end{array}\right] \end{bmatrix}\left[\begin{array}{c} a_1 \\ \vdots \\ a_n \end{array}\right] \amp \amp \knowl{./knowl/column_vector_multiplication.html}{\text{(2.5.6)}}\\ \amp = \mat{P}_{\basis{C} \leftarrow \basis{B}} [\ve{v}]_\basis{B} \, \text{.} \end{align*}
Voorbeeld 2.5.8

In ons deurlopende voorbeeld, sê die stelling dat vir enige vektor \(\ve{v} \in \mathbb{R}^2\text{,}\)

\begin{equation*} [\ve{v}]_\basis{C} = \begin{bmatrix} 1 & 2 \\ 3 & 3 \end{bmatrix} [\ve{v}]_\basis{B} . \end{equation*}

Dit geld dan ook vir ons vektor \(\ve{w}\text{,}\) waarvan die koördinate in die basis \(\ve{B}\) is:

\begin{equation*} [\ve{w}]_\basis{B} = \left[\begin{array}{c} -3 \\ 2 \end{array}\right]\text{.} \end{equation*}

So in hierdie geval sê die stelling dat

\begin{equation*} [\ve{w}]_{\basis{C}} = \begin{bmatrix} 1 & 2 \\ 3 & 3 \end{bmatrix} \left[\begin{array}{c} -3 \\ 2\end{array}\right] = \left[ \begin{array}{c} 1 \\ -3 \end{array} \right] \end{equation*}

wat met ons vorige berekening (2.5.5) ooreenstem!
