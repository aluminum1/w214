We can regard a polynomial \(p\) in two variables as a special kind of function

\begin{equation*} p : \mathbb{R}^2 \rightarrow \mathbb{R}. \end{equation*}

In this way, we can regard \(\Poly_2\) (and similarly \(\Poly^2_n\)) as a subset of the vector space \(\Fun(\mathbb{R}^2)\) of all functions on \(\mathbb{R}^2\text{.}\) Indeed, they are subspaces, as the reader will check.
