Closed under addition.

Suppose \(\ve{w}, \ve{w}' \in W\text{.}\)

That is, \(\ve{v} \dotp \ve{w} = 0 \) and \(\ve{v} \dotp \ve{w}' = 0 \text{.}\)

We must show that \(\ve{w} + \ve{w}' \in W\text{.}\)

That is, we must show that \(\ve{v} \dotp (\ve{w} + \ve{w}') = 0 \text{.}\)

Indeed,

\begin{align*} \ve{v} \dotp (\ve{w} + \ve{w}) \amp = \ve{v} \dotp \ve{w} + \ve{v} \dotp \ve{w}' \\ \amp = 0 + 0 \\ \amp = 0. \end{align*}
Contains the zero vector.

Since \(\ve{v} \dotp \ve{0} = 0\text{,}\) we conclude that \(\ve{0} \in W\text{.}\)

Closed under scalar multiplication.

Suppose \(\ve{w} \in W\) and \(k\) is a scalar.

That is, \(\ve{v} \dotp \ve{w} = 0 \text{.}\)

We must show that \(k \smul \ve{w} \in W\text{.}\)

That is, we must show that \(\ve{v} \dotp (k \smul \ve{w}) = 0 \text{.}\)

Indeed,

\begin{align*} \ve{v} \dotp (k \smul \ve{w}) \amp = k \smul (\ve{v} \dotp \ve{w}) \\ \amp = (k)(0) \\ \amp = 0. \end{align*}
