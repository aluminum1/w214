We write \(\Poly^2\) for the set of all polynomials in two variables, and \(\Poly^2_n\) for the set of all two-variable polynomials of total degree less than or equal to \(n\text{.}\) For instance, consier the polynomial

\begin{equation*} p = 5x^3 y^2 - 3xy^7 \end{equation*}

The total degree of \(p\) is 8. So:

  • \(p \in \Poly^2\text{,}\)
  • \(p \in \Poly^2_8\)
  • \(p \in \Poly^2_12\)
  • \(p \ni \Poly_2_7\)
